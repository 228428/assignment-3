Who are the Brokpas?

The Brokpas of Merak–Sakteng are Bhutan’s highland yak herders — moving with seasons, weaving memory into garments, and carrying stories in a language passed mouth-to-mouth.

Life Along the Yak Trail

  1. Early footsteps
  2. Learning the craft
  3. Seasons & routes
  4. Festivals & belonging

Early footsteps

Herding often begins young — steps learned by listening: hoofbeats on frost, bell chimes across the slope, and the morning path unrolling with light.

Learning the craft

Weaving, ropework, cheese-making, weather-lore — skills that stitch resilience. Tools are passed hand to hand; knowledge lives in practice.

Seasons & routes

Transhumance follows grass and meltwater. Routes bend with snow timing, and maps are kept in memory — a landscape studied in motion.

Festivals & belonging

Youth leave to study; many return for festival days — songs, hats, and dance re-tie scattered lives to place. What remains is kept in practice.

New Paths

Many children head to towns for school. Learning widens, distances lengthen. The past does not disappear — it waits in songs and seasonal returns.

What to Keep

Preservation is practice: weaving circles supported, routes remembered, stories recorded with consent, language spoken daily — not only archived.

Routes of Return

Festivals bring return migrations in miniature — a retying of threads: food, dress, dance, and the quiet weight of a yak’s shadow at dusk.
