Resolution Setup I started with a Constant CHOP to set my resolution to 500. This defined how many instances I would generate. I connected it to a Null CHOP for stability.

Render Network Foundation I created a Box SOP and left it as default. Then I added a Transform SOP, where I set the uniform scale to 0.015 to keep the boxes small. I brought this into a Geo COMP, which I planned to animate later.

Lighting & Camera I added a Camera COMP, adjusting the position once I could see the full grid layout. I also added a Light COMP, which I later changed to a cone light to avoid seeing harsh black edges and create a sense of infinite depth.

Render TOP Settings I added a Render TOP, setting the resolution to 1080x1080, pixel format to 16 bit float (RGBA), and anti aliasing to 16x (high)—as much as my GPU could handle.

Post-Processing I added a Luma Blur for subtle softness, then used an RGB Key to quickly remove the background and make it fully black. A final Null TOP outputted this render.

Position Data from Ramps I created two Ramp TOPs, changed them to RGB: One vertical (Red channel) One horizontal (Green channel) I used Math TOPs to remap the values from 0 - 1 to -5 to 5. This gave me more spatial variation in the X and Z directions. I added a Reorder TOP to assign the red and green channels to translate X and translate Z.

Instancing Setup I connected this reordered data to the Instancing OP on the Geo COMP. I set the translate X and Z to be driven by the R and G channels from the ramps.

Fine-Tuning I played with the camera position until I got a nice view of the instanced boxes. I also tweaked the cone light to make the visual field more immersive.
